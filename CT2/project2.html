Dream Space: Maze

For this project, I created a dream space centered around a maze. Inspired by the movie The Maze Runner and my recurring dreams of being trapped inside mazes, I wanted to design an environment full of mystery, gentle confusion, and playful exploration.

Planning & Building

I started by sketching a 20x20 orthogonal maze, carefully arranging the pathways to create a balanced and navigable structure.

Original maze sketch layout.

Instead of manually drawing every wall in Unity, I designed a custom terrain brush based on the maze layout. This allowed me to stamp the entire maze onto the terrain surface efficiently.

Maze stamped onto the terrain using a custom brush.

Starting to decorate the inside of the maze with natural elements.

To create a more lively and natural feeling, I manually painted grass across the maze floor. Although it was time-consuming, this process gave the terrain a rich, layered texture.

Hand-painting grass onto the terrain.

Top-down view after grass painting was completed.

I then constructed the maze entrance, adding small structures and natural decorations to guide the players' first impression.

Constructing the maze entrance area.

Challenges & Adaptations

One major challenge was texturing the maze walls. Initially, I tried applying a detailed soil texture, but it stretched and distorted badly over the vertical surfaces, ruining the visual consistency. To fix this, I replaced complex textures with a simple brown material and strategically planted trees to mask the terrain edges, creating a softer, more immersive environment.

This experience taught me that sometimes adapting the strategy — focusing less on textures and more on environmental storytelling — can lead to better results.

Experience & Reflection

To make the maze explorable, I added a mini First Person Controller in Unity. No complex physics systems were necessary beyond the default collision and gravity provided by Unity. Moving through the maze from a first-person perspective enhanced the sense of immersion and scale.

The biggest takeaway from this project was learning to adapt my original vision based on technical limitations. In the future, I would love to develop a dynamic maze where paths could shift and change as players explore — creating a truly living, breathing dream space.
